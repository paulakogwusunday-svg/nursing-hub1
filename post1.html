What is NANDA in Nursing and How to Use It

NANDA stands for North American Nursing Diagnosis Association. It is a standardized classification system for nursing diagnoses that helps nurses identify and address patient health problems effectively. NANDA provides a common language for nurses to communicate about patient care and ensures consistency in nursing practice.

How to Use NANDA in Nursing:

  • Assessment: Begin by conducting a thorough assessment of the patient, including physical, psychological, social, and environmental factors.
  • Identify Health Problems: Based on the assessment data, identify the patient's health problems or potential health risks.
  • Formulate Nursing Diagnoses: Use the NANDA taxonomy to formulate nursing diagnoses that accurately reflect the patient's health problems. Each diagnosis consists of a diagnostic label, related factors, and defining characteristics.
  • Plan Care: Develop a care plan based on the identified nursing diagnoses. This plan should include specific goals, interventions, and expected outcomes.
  • Implement Interventions: Carry out the planned nursing interventions to address the identified health problems.
  • Evaluate Outcomes: Continuously evaluate the patient's response to interventions and adjust the care plan as needed.
