What is NANDA in Nursing?

NANDA stands for North American Nursing Diagnosis Association. It is an organization that develops standardized nursing diagnoses used by nurses worldwide.

A Simple Guide to Understanding NANDA in Nursing Diagnosis

In nursing, a nursing diagnosis is a clinical judgment about a patient's response to health conditions or life processes. NANDA provides an official list of approved nursing diagnoses to help nurses plan proper care.

Why is NANDA Important?

  • Provides standardized nursing language
  • Improves communication among nurses
  • Guides nursing care planning
  • Helps in writing care plans correctly

Example of a NANDA Nursing Diagnosis

Impaired Gas Exchange related to alveolar-capillary membrane changes as evidenced by shortness of breath and low oxygen saturation.

Simple Breakdown

  • Problem – What is wrong? (Impaired Gas Exchange)
  • Cause – Why is it happening?
  • Evidence – Signs and symptoms observed

In short, NANDA helps nurses identify patient problems and create effective care plans.
