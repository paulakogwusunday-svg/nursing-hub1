Omeprazole vs Cimetidine

Drug Class

  • Omeprazole – Proton Pump Inhibitor (PPI)
  • Cimetidine – H2 Receptor Antagonist (H2 Blocker)

🔬 Mechanism of Action

🟡 Omeprazole (PPI)

Blocks the proton pump (H⁺/K⁺ ATPase) in the stomach lining. This pump is the final step in acid production.

Result

Very strong acid suppression. Think of it as turning off the acid factory completely.

🔵 Cimetidine (H2 Blocker)

Blocks H2 receptors on stomach cells. Histamine normally stimulates acid production. When blocked → less acid is produced.

Think of it as reducing acid signals, not completely shutting it down.

📚 Simplified Comparison

Feature Omeprazole Cimetidine
Strength Stronger Moderate
Duration Longer Shorter
Onset Slower Faster
Use Severe GERD, ulcers Mild GERD, heartburn

💊 Common Uses

  • GERD (acid reflux)
  • Peptic ulcer disease
  • Gastritis
  • Zollinger-Ellison syndrome

⚠️ Side Effects

Common (Omeprazole)

  1. Headache
  2. Nausea
  3. Diarrhea
  4. Abdominal pain

Long-Term Risks (Omeprazole)

  • Vitamin B12 deficiency
  • Hypomagnesemia
  • Increased fracture risk
  • Risk of C. difficile infection

🔵 Cimetidine Side Effects

Common:

  • Dizziness
  • Diarrhea
  • Headache

Important Unique Effects:

  • Gynecomastia
  • Reduced libido
  • Impotence
  • Confusion (especially in elderly)
  • Many drug interactions (CYP450 inhibition)

🏥 Nursing Responsibilities

For Omeprazole

  • Give before meals (usually morning)
  • Do NOT crush enteric-coated tablets
  • Monitor magnesium if long-term use
  • Assess for GI bleeding

For Cimetidine

  • Monitor mental status (elderly)
  • Check for drug interactions
  • Monitor liver function
  • Assess hormonal side effects
