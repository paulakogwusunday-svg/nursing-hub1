How to Write a Nursing Care Plan

A nursing care plan is a structured guide used by nurses to identify patient problems, set goals, and provide appropriate nursing interventions.

A Step-by-Step Guide to Writing a Nursing Care Plan

Step 1: Assessment

Collect complete data about the patient. This includes:

  • Vital signs
  • Medical history
  • Physical examination findings
  • Patient complaints
  • Laboratory results

Step 2: Nursing Diagnosis

Identify the patient’s problem using approved nursing diagnoses (such as NANDA diagnoses).

Example:
Impaired physical mobility related to pain as evidenced by difficulty walking.

Step 3: Goal Setting (Planning)

Set clear and measurable goals for the patient. Goals should be:

  • Specific
  • Measurable
  • Achievable
  • Realistic
  • Time-bound (SMART)

Step 4: Nursing Interventions

List the actions the nurse will take to help achieve the goals.

  • Monitor vital signs regularly
  • Administer prescribed medications
  • Assist with mobility
  • Provide patient education

Step 5: Evaluation

Determine whether the patient’s goals were achieved. If not, modify the care plan as needed.

Simple Format of a Nursing Care Plan

  • Assessment
  • Nursing Diagnosis
  • Goal/Outcome
  • Interventions
  • Evaluation

Writing a nursing care plan helps nurses provide organized, patient-centered, and effective care.
